Chilli's

We use a broad range of chilli's from all over the world to capture our unique flavours All ranging in different heat and bringing there own distinct flavours.

Habeanero

capsicum chinense

200,000 – 580,000 Scoville Units. The Red Savina pepper is a cultivar of the habanero pepper, which has been selectively bred to produce hotter, heavier, and larger fruit.

  • Scovilles: 580000
  • Origin: USA

Padron Pepper

capsicum annuum

500 – 2,500 Scoville Units. A variety of peppers from the municipality of Padrón in northwestern Spain. These are small peppers (about 5 cm/2” long), with a color ranging from bright green to yellowish green.

  • Scovilles:
  • Origin: Spain

Jalapeño

capsicum annuum

2,500 – 10,000 Scoville Units. The Jalapeño is the world’s most popular chili pepper. A medium-sized chili that is commonly picked and consumed while still green, but if allowed to fully ripen they turn crimson red.

  • Scovilles: 10000
  • Origin: Mexico

Cayenne

capsicum annuum

30,000 – 50,000 Scoville Units. The cayenne pepper, also known as the Guinea spice, cow-horn pepper, aleva, bird pepper, or, especially in its powdered form, red pepper, is a hot chile pepper used to flavor many dishes.

  • Scovilles: 50000
  • Origin: French Guyana
